Matia Paki - Project 1

Nice project and very impactful subject. In your text you said you wanted to focus more on the concept and keep a basic visual. Your stripped-down visuals work very well for your very informative approach. You are not there to stun people with your visuals, you are there to inform. 

I like the small addition of the white underline at the base of the trees. 

I fully agree with your statement about our digital imprint consuming our natural resources. The worst part is that this race for AI digital is made by companies that don’t share their facilities. This means that instead of a shared single data center, they all have their own centers that each draws their own water. This is a clear example of human greed, something I find also in your project. You are kind of encouraged to plant trees and it seems like there is no limit to your creativity and your desires but then at some point, when you decide to put too many trees, you are punished for your greed and everything crumbles. There is no clear warning to prevent you from planting too much. Yes the scene gets a bit darker but you tell yourself it’s just a cloud it’s no big deal. You are the only one that can tell yourself that it’s time to stop planting, there is just the right amount of trees, no need to put more or they will have no personal space. 

The things I would say could be improved is your code that makes the scene darker. Right now, the logic is that it increases by x amount instantly each time a tree is planted.Maybe make a fade in instead of an instant trigger. 

You could make the trigger for the critical state a random number between 15 and X, because the second time I play the game I realize that I can’t go past 15 or else I trigger the critical state. Making it random could keep me guessing and maybe I’ll try to push my luck because I am greedy just like everyone else. This would reinforce the idea that we are really poking the bear with this natural resource situation.  

Also, I can only end the game with the space button. I can click forever even though the screen has reached its fully black limit and never trigger the end but as soon as I press space it shows me the end text. 






Benjamin Mehri - Project 1

The UI is great, I see that you like this part of the process and take time to polish it. Everything is clear and the visual elements such as the small instructions and the score explains everything you need to know. 

Having talked to Benjamin in class about his idea of the project, I fully agree with his take that there are really too many pop up ads on websites. It makes everything crowded and ugly. Everything is made with the goal to squeeze every last drop of your attention until you give up and just accept that a bombardment of ads is just something you will accept even though you and the ad creator both know it’s so annoying. 

The gameplay loop really reinforces the thematic you are trying to show. These days, ads are really the bane of our digital experience. They are seeping in every part of the net so that you never leave your eyes and attention from them. You may let the user some freedom and the ability to run from these ads but it is all illusory. In real life, you may say today, you will get an adblock or you will ignore all ads that are being shown to you. Well, good luck with that, easier said than done. Even if you manage to not see any ads on the digital space, you will get ads on billboards on the road, on the train or the metro and at school. There is so much to be done in ads that companies will make sure that there is no way to not see ads. All this also works hand in hand with our attention overload. Your code is made so that you are sure to see ads in front of you and you accept that you are the one that will need to comply or else you are done. 

Moving the ball is stressful and you are engaged with the game because you genuinely don’t want to get caught by those annoying ads. The small box also reinforces the entrapment you feel in real life like there is nowhere to hide from these ads that act as aggressively as viruses.    

I would say the thing that could be improved is that right now you can last around up to a 70-80 score based on the way the code is made. After that whether you are a good or bad player it becomes impossible to stay alive. Maybe a good improvement could be to add some randomness to the rhythm between each reappearance of the pop up ads to keep the user on their toes. It would not change the fact that eventually you end up losing but it could increase the replayability. It’s really not a primordial thing to do since the certain incoming death is already strong and present but it could finetune and increase the duration that the user is glued to the screen.
